Cleaning Our City's Air, One Particle at a Time.

Welcome!

This is the digital companion for my EVS project, "The Urban Lung." This project features a working model of a multi-stage air filter designed to show how we can trap harmful pollutants from the air we breathe. But a model is just the beginning.

Here, we will explore the science behind the filter, connect it to the real-world challenge of air pollution in our city, Kolkata, and discover the steps we can all take towards a healthier future.

A Look Inside: The Science of Filtration

My model uses a three-stage process to clean the air, with each layer targeting a different type of pollutant. This is the same principle used in high-tech air purifiers.

1

Pre-Filter (Cotton Wool)

This layer serves as the crucial first line of defense. It uses **mechanical filtration** to trap the largest airborne particles—things we can often see, like road dust, construction debris, pollen, and hair. By removing these larger contaminants first, the pre-filter prevents the more sensitive inner layers from getting clogged, dramatically increasing their lifespan and efficiency.

2

Adsorption Filter (Activated Charcoal)

This layer works through a chemical process called **adsorption**, where pollutant molecules stick to its surface. Activated charcoal is treated to create millions of microscopic pores, giving it an incredibly large surface area. This acts like a sponge for harmful gases, traffic fumes, cooking smells, and Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs), effectively removing odours and invisible chemical threats from the air.

3

Fine Particle Filter (Mask Cloth)

This is the most critical layer for protecting our health. It's designed to trap the most dangerous microscopic particles, especially **PM2.5** (particles less than 2.5 micrometers wide, or 30 times smaller than a human hair). These particles can bypass our body's natural defenses and enter deep into our lungs and bloodstream, causing serious health issues. This filter acts as a fine net, capturing these invisible dangers.

The Big Picture: Kolkata's Live Air Dashboard

My model cleans a small amount of air. But what is the condition of the air across our entire city right now? This live widget shows the real-time Air Quality Index (AQI).

Live Air Quality Index (AQI) for Kolkata

The AQI is like a thermometer for air pollution, running from 0 to 500. This widget shows the live data from a monitoring station in Kolkata. The colour tells you the level of health risk at a glance.

What's in Our Air?

The AQI number you see is caused by several factors in our city:

  • ➤Vehicular Emissions: Exhaust from buses, cars, and autos.
  • ➤Construction Dust: From the Metro and new buildings.
  • ➤Industrial Pollution: From factories in and around the city.
  • ➤Seasonal Factors: In winter, cool air traps pollutants, making the air quality much worse.

From Prototype to Planet: Scaling Up the Solution

My small model demonstrates a powerful principle. But how can this technology be applied on a large scale to make a real difference in a city like Kolkata?

Industrial Chimney Filters

Factories and power plants can be fitted with large-scale "scrubbers" and electrostatic precipitators. These are giant versions of my filter that can remove over 99% of particulate matter and harmful gases before they ever enter our atmosphere.

Urban Air Purification Towers

Some cities are experimenting with massive outdoor air purifiers, often called "smog towers." These towers draw in polluted city air, filter it using multiple layers just like my model, and release cleaner air back into the environment.

Advanced Building Filtration (HVAC)

Every large modern building, like malls, offices, and hospitals, uses a centralized HVAC system with high-efficiency filters. This creates safe indoor air quality, protecting thousands of people from outdoor pollution daily.

From Problem to Action

Air pollution has real effects on our health, but the solution requires action from all of us. Here's how we can contribute.

Personal Actions

  • Use Public Transport (Metro/Bus)
  • Wear an N95 mask on high AQI days
  • Conserve electricity at home
  • Add air-purifying indoor plants

Community Actions

  • Plant trees in your neighbourhood
  • Spread awareness with friends and family
  • Never burn trash or leaves
  • Report vehicles emitting excessive smoke
